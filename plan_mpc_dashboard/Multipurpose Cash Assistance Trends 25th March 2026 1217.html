📊 CALP's Analysis

As of March 2026, with the majority of humanitarian country response plans published, MPC was 5.7% of the total humanitarian requirements, down slightly from 5.8% in 2025. Given that cash is more efficient to deliver, preferred by recipients and MPC in particular was prioritised in the Reset and supported by major donors you'd expect a spike – not a plateau.

While underreporting of MPC funding has been an ongoing issue, what is clear is that implementers and response planning teams are not prioritising MPC. Whether this reflects reluctance to include MPC, a shift toward sectoral cash, or a broader move away from cash and voucher assistance is unclear from the current data.

Our analysis uses data published by OCHA's Financial Tracking Service (FTS) on their web pages and accessed through their API. We plan to update this dashboard periodically if users find it useful. Please share any feedback or let us know if you spot errors by clicking the Provide feedback button.
